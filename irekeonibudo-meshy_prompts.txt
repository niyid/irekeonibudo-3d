IREKEONIBUDO 3D — MESHY / TRIPO PROMPT LIST
==============================================
Matches GameObjects created in Assets/Editor/SceneSetupWizard.cs.
Source: Ìrèké Oníbùdó (1949) by D.O. Fagunwa — a shipwrecked traveler in
Arogidigba's undersea kingdom, guided by his dead mother's spirit.

Generate in the priority order listed. Set Pose = T-Pose for items marked
[RIGGED] since those need animation. Same workflow as ogbojuode-3d: prefer
Smart Mesh over HD Model for game-ready topology on a mobile target.


1. Player_Ireke_Onibudo  [RIGGED — CHARACTER]
------------------------------------------------
Full-body 3D game character, a young 19th-century Yoruba traveler,
shipwrecked and soaked, wearing a simple woven wrapper cloth and a torn
shirt. Lean build, alert and wary expression, dark skin, short natural
hair, barefoot. Carrying a plain wooden blade at the hip. Stylized
dark-fantasy game-ready model, clean quad topology, T-pose, ready for
auto-rigging.


2. Arogidigba_Mermaid_Queen  [RIGGED — RIDDLE BOSS]
-------------------------------------------------------
A regal mermaid queen, human from the waist up and a large fish from the
waist down, pale greenish-blue skin with a faint scale pattern climbing
her arms and shoulders, long dark hair drifting as if underwater, an
ornate coral crown, calm and commanding expression rather than monstrous.
Mystical, dignified bearing, stylized realism, game-ready, T-pose.


3. Warrior_Fish_Eja_Jagunjagun  [TITAN — TOWERING GUARD]
-------------------------------------------------------------
A towering humanoid fish-warrior guarding an undersea kingdom, thick
armored scales in deep blue and silver, gilled neck, clawed webbed
hands gripping a coral-tipped spear, hulking intimidating posture.
Afro-surrealist dark-fantasy creature, highly detailed, game-ready,
T-pose or idle stance.


4. Wrestler_Cat_Ologbo_Ijakadi  [BRUTE — HULKING]

A hulking anthropomorphic wrestler-cat creature, thick muscular build

SKIP — DO NOT GENERATE ON MESHY
==================================
Seabed_Floor / Village_Ground
  -> Terrain, not a discrete mesh. Use Unity Terrain tool + tiling
     ground/sand texture instead.

Thrown spear projectile in flight
  -> Too small/fast to read as a detailed mesh in motion once thrown.
     Use a plain capsule or sphere + particle trail instead — same
     reasoning as ogbojuode-3d's musket bullet.

Spear_Launch_Point
  -> Empty anchor Transform in code, not a visible object.


NOTES
======
- Free tier: prioritize items 1-6 (character/creature cast) first, since
  those are highest-risk for needing a re-prompt; batch items 13-15
  (coral, plankton, reeds) together as a low-detail "cheap win" pass.
- Items 7-12 (spear, blade, hut, canoe, dock, shrine) are close enough to
  generic library props that Kenney/Poly Haven CC0 kits are worth trying
  FIRST before spending Tripo credits — see ogbojuode-3d's prop-sourcing
  precedent (Nature Kit, Survival Kit, Fantasy Town Kit all have usable
  stand-ins for huts/docks/props). Only fall back to a custom Tripo
  generation if the generic result doesn't read as "Yoruba riverside"
  after texturing.
- Export format: FBX or GLB, both import cleanly into Unity.
- Items marked [RIGGED] should use Meshy/Tripo's auto-rig feature after
  generation (T-pose export required for this to work).
- On import, rescale so Warrior_Fish_Eja_Jagunjagun reads as noticeably
  larger than Ireke Onibudo, matching the (2.5, 3.5, 4) scale already set
  in SceneSetupWizard.cs.
- Copyright/licensing note carried over from ogbojuode-3d: AI-generated
  3D asset ownership and licensing terms are still unsettled. Treat
  everything produced from this list as prototype-grade until revisited
  on a paid/commercial-clear tier or replaced with custom/CC0 work before
  anything ships.
